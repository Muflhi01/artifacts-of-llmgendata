Abstract

The emergence of Large Language Models (LLMs) like ChatGPT has revolutionized research and practical applications, necessitating a comprehensive understanding of their capabilities and limitations. This survey conducts an in-depth analysis of LLMs, focusing on their safety, reliability, and potential risks, including a systematic literature review across various data types. Our study is the first to critically analyze a range of LLM-generated data, identifying common patterns and biases, and proposing new evaluation metrics beyond traditional standards. Key findings include the distinctiveness of human-authored texts, biases in synthetic datasets, and the need for diverse metrics in assessing LLMs. Our findings highlight the importance of nuanced understanding in domains like sentiment analysis and the generalizability issues in training models. This analysis forms the basis for developing universal evaluation protocols, crucial for advancing LLM research and ensuring the reliability of their generated data across diverse applications.
